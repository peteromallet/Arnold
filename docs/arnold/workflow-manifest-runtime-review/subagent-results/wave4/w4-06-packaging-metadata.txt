### 5. Key Gaps Summary

1. **No `arnold_pipelines` namespace package** — M4 must introduce it with `py.typed` and wheel config.
2. **No `console_scripts` entrypoints** — `pyproject.toml` has zero entrypoint definitions; M5 CLI specs require them.
3. **No `arnold.workflow`, `arnold.kernel`, `arnold.execution`, or `arnold.patterns` packages** — all six milestones are greenfield at the neutral-core layer.
4. **Existing `arnold.pipeline` is product-contaminated** — `executor.py`, `builder.py`, and `native/` contain Megaplan-coupled logic that must be disentangled or deleted.
5. **No golden/parity fixtures** defined at `tests/fixtures/workflow/` — M1 must establish behavior baselines before any migration.
6. **No installed-wheel conformance scaffolding** — M1 requires it; M6 enforces it as a hard gate.
7. **`pyproject.toml` wheel `packages` only lists `["arnold"]`** — must expand to include `arnold_pipelines` when introduced in M4.
8. **Review artifacts directory** (`docs/arnold/workflow-manifest-runtime-review/`) is planned but not populated — wave results reference paths that don't yet exist in the repo.
9. **No `arnold_pipelines/megaplan`** — the permanent product home is greenfield; current product lives at the to-be-deleted `arnold/pipelines/megaplan`.
10. **Dependency groups**: `db` and `agent` extras exist but no `workflow`, `execution`, or `pipelines` optional-dependency groups are defined — the plan adds no new extras.
