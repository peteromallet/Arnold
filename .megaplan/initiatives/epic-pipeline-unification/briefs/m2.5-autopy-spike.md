## Locked decisions

- **Pure characterization.** The spike records `drive()`'s behavior AS-IS. It fixes NO bugs ‚Äî the
  `DEFAULT_MAX_BLOCKED_RETRIES=1` issue (MEMORY: chain-blocked-retry) and the idle-stall history are
  captured as recorded behavior + noted in the decision doc; their fix is M3/M4's RecoveryPolicy.
- **Lands on `main`, not a worktree.** Permanent CI in `tests/characterization/` ‚Äî the corpus must run
  against every one of the repo's ~80‚Äì900 commits/wk (PROGRAM.md:100-103) and against M3's flip.
- **The `_run_megaplan` seam is the recording boundary** ‚Äî drive subprocess output deterministically
  by scripting that seam (the existing `test_auto.py` pattern), so the corpus is hermetic and replays
  byte-stable in CI without spawning real model calls.
- **The corpus is THE behavioral-replay oracle** (PROGRAM.md:136-139, 372, 381-385). M3's
  substrate-swap oracle (resume-across-version, crash-isolation) replays the recorded
  blocked-retry-then-resume trace across the R1 authority boundary (PROGRAM.md:434).
- **Resume-model decision = a DEFAULT, decided now.** The chosen model is `current_state` +
  `resume_cursor{phase, retry_strategy}` as the single recorded resume fact (it is the encoding every
  failure site already writes); `STATE_AWAITING_HUMAN` stays a *terminal-state value*, not a parallel
  resume channel; M3's realized-graph projection (`next_step`) is DERIVED, not stored
  (REGISTER ¬ßM3, M6 `workflow_next` resolution at REGISTER:112). The two step-layer channels ‚Äî
  `_pipeline_paused_stage` (`human_gate.py:94` write / `run_cli.py:267` read / `cli/__init__.py:951` pop)
  and `awaiting_user.json` (`executor.py:264,376` write / `resume.py:104` check / `run_cli.py:271`
  reload) ‚Äî are reconciled INTO this single fact: the M5c F6 clarify-resume cursor (`awaiting_user.json`)
  and the human-gate paused-stage marker become projections of `current_state` + `resume_cursor`, not
  independent resume channels. Four channels in, ONE model out.
