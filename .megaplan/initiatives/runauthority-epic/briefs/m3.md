# Sprint 3 - Consumer Migration And Compatibility Cleanup

## Outcome

Move the authority system from foundation/enforcement spine into practical consumers:

1. implement sibling operational views for runner, publication, human gates, and recovery;
2. rewire major consumers through shadow, warn, and enforce modes where practical;
3. prove generic kernel reuse with a second consumer or document the precise blocker;
4. remove, quarantine, or demote duplicate raw-artifact authority readers to compatibility projections.

- `.megaplan/initiatives/runauthority-epic/decisions/runauthority-architecture-decision.md` - rationale for narrow kernel and sibling views.

Inspect Sprint 1 and Sprint 2 outputs before planning. This sprint should consume the contracts, validators, and authority views that already exist rather than redesigning them.

## Scope

In scope:

- Implement or complete `RunnerView`, `PublicationView`, `HumanGateView`, `MegaplanRecoveryView`, and the `MegaplanPlanView` facade.
- Separate execution authority, runner liveness, publication state, human gates, and recovery custody in status/debug output.
- Rewire execute scheduler, merge/reconcile, chain advancement, cloud status, watchdog, repair loop, PR/publication wait handling, and human override handling through shadow/warn/enforce where practical.
- Add diagnostics when legacy/raw artifact interpretation disagrees with authority views, including source paths and reasons.
- Apply the generic kernel to one non-Megaplan consumer where feasible, or produce a precise design note explaining why the second-consumer proof must follow this epic.
- Delete, quarantine, or demote duplicate raw authority readers after equivalence tests pass.
- Add tests for runner identity mismatch, stale active-step, orphan runner, branch ancestry, auth/push/PR publication classification, human decision view-hash binding, stale override supersession, repair custody routing, chain child-run aggregation, view hash determinism, event replay determinism, duplicate idempotency, and crash/restart replay where practical.

Out of scope:

- New feature work unrelated to authority.
- A universal lifecycle enum.
- Promoting Megaplan-only semantics into the generic kernel without a proven second consumer.

## Done Criteria

- Operator-facing status/debug can show execution, runner, publication, gates, and recovery separately.
- Major consumers no longer independently interpret raw artifacts for authority when an authority view is available.
- Shadow/warn/enforce rollout gates and rollback behavior are documented.
- Compatibility projections remain only as projections or explicitly quarantined legacy inputs.
- Duplicate raw authority readers are removed or tracked as follow-up with exact blockers.
- The epic leaves a final migration/cleanup report listing completed enforcement surfaces, remaining compatibility surfaces, and evidence/tests.
